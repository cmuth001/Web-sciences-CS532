all about her

i pull the envelope from the box and my mouth goes dry. i open it and struggle to remember how to read. scolding myself i make out the words.

eve.

no. god. not eve.

i blink. when did my head start pounding? 

well. merry fucking christmas.

with every obstacle, every hurdle, every family member or friend, every disagreement lost or won; it always comes back to eve.

at the all night grocery store i wander aimlessly. i came out for soda and salad and butterscotch chips. i forgot all three.  i remembered the booze though.

i could just keep driving. few would blame me. i crank up the radio. the beat rumbling steadily in my chest is assuring.

i turn off everything that isn't the beat.

i sit in the car, just this side of too long then go back inside.  just shouldn't think anymore tonight.

Saturday, July 15, 2017

wasted youth

youth really is wasted on the young.

i was so nervous about everything when i was young.  now that i'm older and i realize that the 'weight of my actions' is only the weight i give it, and that i can afford to steer off into the distance, it's hard to find anyone to go with me.  lol.  or maybe i'm judged. 

sometimes i feel like i missed something by settling down so soon.  not that i really did it quickly, but there really weren't many 'trials.'  and by that i mean, well frankly i never sowed my wild oats.  i never got around, i never threw caution to the wind and did something just for the feel of it. 

and now that i feel seasoned enough to do such things, i'm someones wife.  someones mom.  someones dependable someone.

i can see why 'christians,' and those of us raised as such who can't shake the feeling, cheat.  we spend our lives doing good, doing right, following the rules (even if we set the rules ourselves.)  then when it's time to cut loose, we either don't, or we go absolutely bananas.  le sigh. 

first world problems.

Monday, January 23, 2017

vodka and lolipops

i love my son every day, but some days i can feel my hair turning white.

when he was a baby i worried about giving in to him too quickly.  i worried about him learning to manipulate me.  and now i look back at those worries, i look back at the mother i was and i laugh.  she has no idea what manipulation is. 

have you ever dealt with a 5 year old?  they are beasts.  imagine, really.  they have the tools of a full grown person, the unabashed greed of a child, and next to zero fear of retaliation.  they do as they please, knowing they can get away with it.  unless of course the parent is willing to turn the tiff into an all day war. 

in order to win a fight with a child, you have to act like a child.  decide what you want and do not give in until you get it.  do not compromise.  do not falter, oh no, they can smell that and they will fucking eat you alive. 

it seems the only way i can edge towards raising my son to be anything other than a little asshole is to be the big asshole myself.  to be the dictator i loathed when i was growing up.  it's a real changing of gears that i can barely get my head around, let alone master. 

it's true what they say, that your child doesn't need a friend but a parent.  they need you to be the authoritarian.  you have to be the fucking law.  and it only makes sense, really.  you have to teach them what other people won't bother to teach them.  that when they go out in world acting like little assholes they will get nowhere.  fast.  and alone.  it really is the hardest job you'll ever have, and the most thankless.

and most days they turn your hair white and drive you to drink.  but then later that day, or the next, they do something so amazing that you can feel your heart swell like the grinch giving cindy loo who back her christmas gifts.  hold on to those moments, mama.  hold on to those few and far between feelings of pride and the notion that you are in fact doing something right.  you'll need them.

sam and dean. or... dean anyway...

“What are you gonna say?” Dean asks. “That you were the greatest boyfriend alive. That you were generous and protective and kind and strong. That as much as I wish we’d met when I was older and more mature, I know that if I hadn’t had you with me when I did, I wouldn’t be the person I am today. That you taught me what safe feels like,”

so.  much.  yes.

i hope you were all lucky enough to have a Dean.  i did.  or rather, i had a joel.  and there are days of my life where i wish i could tell him.  hell there are days i wish i could take it all back and never leave.  but we all know life doesn't work in reverse.  like Rory said, if we took back everything we view in hindsight as a mistake, we wouldn't be who we've come to be.  

if my dad weren't such a jackass, i wouldn't be able to appreciate kindness.  i wouldn't have the strength i have.  if i hadn't met joel i might not understand love, and protection, and selflessness.  if i hadn't left joel i wouldn't ever have appreciated him.  i was spoiled.  and without both of those things, i wouldn't have been able to walk the tightrope i walk every day between love and respect with my husband.  not that i don't ever fall right off.

'would you forsake your house and home?  would you forsake your baby?  would you forsake your husband too, to go with black jack davey?'


and maybe without having met my demanding, spoiled, entitled self; joel wouldn't have known how to give without giving all. 

as per usual, i digress.

the alcohol is strong with this one. 

the point, i guess, is that ... fuck  ... balls .. i don't even know anymore.
