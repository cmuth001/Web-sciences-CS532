Leeds United owner Andrea Radrizzani last night admitted he made a “mistake” in appointing rookie boss Thomas Christiansen.

\n\n\t\t\t\t

The Leeds owner ended Christiansen\xe2\x80\x99s 35-game reign as head coach on Sunday, 24 hours after a crushing 4-1 home defeat to Cardiff City.

\n \n
\n
\n
\n\n\n\n
\n
\n
\n

Radrizzani said he had given Christiansen \xe2\x80\x9cmany chances\xe2\x80\x9d to prove himself and pointed to Leeds\xe2\x80\x99s FA Cup defeat to League Two side Newport County last month as the result which ended his faith in Dane.

\n
\n
\n

Paul Heckingbottom was named as his replacement within 48 hours, arriving from Barnsley after Leeds paid \xc2\xa3500,000 to buy him out of his contract at Oakwell.

\n
\n
\n

Radrizzani accused Christiansen of failing \xe2\x80\x9cin terms of communication, leadership and confidence\xe2\x80\x9d and apologised to the club\xe2\x80\x99s support for gambling on a coach whose prior experience consisted of three years spent managing in Cyprus.

\xe2\x80\x9cAnything that doesn\xe2\x80\x99t work is a mistake so yes, I did make a mistake,\xe2\x80\x9d Radrizzani said. \xe2\x80\x9cI apologise to the fans and the club, and for my experience I need to learn and analyse better my choice before making it.

\n
\n

I waited for a long time before taking the decision and gave him many chances. Already at the beginning of January I was really keen to change. Victor (Orta, Leeds’s director of football) convinced me to hold on and give him one more

\n\nAndrea Radrizzani\n
\n\n

\xe2\x80\x9cAt the same time, he seemed to be someone successful and fresh who could match with the new phase of the club, my new management and the new freshness around the team. But if you watch the games we conceded so many chances early on, in the first few minutes. This is a sign that mentally the players are not relaxed. They are stressed and this is why I could feel that probably the manager was not transmitting security and confidence.

\xe2\x80\x9cI waited for a long time before taking the decision and gave him many chances. Already at the beginning of January I was really keen to change. Victor (Orta, Leeds\xe2\x80\x99s director of football) convinced me to hold on and give him one more, one more, one more; extra chances but at the end, something was missing.\xe2\x80\x9d

\n
\n
\n
\n
\n
\n

Radrizzani added: \xe2\x80\x9cWe need to share this responsibility but probably the job was a little bit too big for him at this point in his career. I wish him good luck and success and he will have the time to grow but at the same time I think this league is competitive, very difficult and he probably lacked a little bit in terms of knowledge of the football.\xe2\x80\x9d
