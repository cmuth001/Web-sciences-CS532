Kolby Listenbee\xe2\x80\x99s personal injury lawsuit against TCU, football coach Gary Patterson and others has created a firestorm of opinions.

\n \n \n \n \n

A few of his former teammates have ripped Listenbee for filing the lawsuit, while others have voiced their support. Some believe Listenbee, an Arlington Bowie product, is just trying to find an excuse for what\xe2\x80\x99s been a failed NFL career to this point.

\n \n \n \n \n

But experts believe this legal battle could turn into a high-stakes game for all involved.

\n \n \n \n \n

\xe2\x80\x9cOne of the big issues right now in all of athletics is Toradol and other injections like that which are given to players before every game when they\xe2\x80\x99re only supposed to be used once every three months,\xe2\x80\x9d said Rogge Dunn, a Dallas-based attorney who successfully represented Texas Tech in the $12.5 million wrongful termination lawsuit filed by former football coach Mike Leach in 2010.

\n \n \n
\n \n\n\n\n

\xe2\x80\x9cThis is prevalent and this lawsuit can have merit. Coaches telling kids to play through injuries, bullying kids into playing by saying, \xe2\x80\x98You\xe2\x80\x99re not tough enough. The sissy line is over there.\xe2\x80\x99 It\xe2\x80\x99s a real problem.\xe2\x80\x9d

\n \n \n \n \n \n \n
\n
\n
\n\n \n \n \n

The problem for Listenbee, though, is building a strong enough case. People following this case will likely hear the word \xe2\x80\x9ccausation\xe2\x80\x9d hundreds of times.

\n \n \n \n \n

Listenbee has to prove causation in three areas, Dunn said.

\n \n \n \n \n

First, he has to find a medical expert to side with him that he was rushed back to the field. Listenbee sustained a pelvis injury in September 2015, but claims TCU coaches and staff didn\xe2\x80\x99t give him adequate time to let the injury heal. The injury, according to Listenbee\xe2\x80\x99s attorneys, should\xe2\x80\x99ve been given six months to heal.

\n \n \n \n \n

Instead, Listenbee played after three weeks.

\n \n \n \n \n

\xe2\x80\x9cYou\xe2\x80\x99re going to need a doctor to say, \xe2\x80\x98He should\xe2\x80\x99ve sat on the sideline and been given time to allow the injury to naturally heal as opposed to rushing him back and giving him drugs to mask the pain,\xe2\x80\x99 \xe2\x80\x9d Dunn said.

\n \n \n \n \n \n \n
\n
\n
\n\n \n \n \n

If Listenbee makes a compelling case for this, it could become an even larger issue for TCU and Patterson. NCAA rules prohibit \xe2\x80\x9cknowingly providing medications to student-athletes contrary to medical licensure, commonly accepted standards of care in sports medicine practice, or state and federal law.\xe2\x80\x9d

\n \n \n \n \n

The NCAA\xe2\x80\x99s Sports Medicine Handbook discourages extensive use of corticosteroids and numbing agents.

\n \n \n \n \n

The handbook states, \xe2\x80\x9cInjectable corticosteroids should be administered only after more conservative treatments, including nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory agents, rest, ice, ultrasound and various treatment modalities, have been exhausted. Repeated corticosteroid injections at a specific site should be done only after the consequences and benefits of the injections have been thoroughly evaluated.\xe2\x80\x9d

\n \n \n \n \n

After that, Listenbee and his attorneys would need to find a medical expert who agrees permanent damage had been done with him returning too soon and that is the likely cause of why he had to undergo surgery to repair two sports hernias.

\n \n \n \n \n

Finally, Listenbee would have to have an NFL general manager or player personnel guru state that his injuries caused him to fall in the draft. This becomes even more precarious given the number of players drafted in the NFL who go through a similar career path as Listenbee has gone through.
