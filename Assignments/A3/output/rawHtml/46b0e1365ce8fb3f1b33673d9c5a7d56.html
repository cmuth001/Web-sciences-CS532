Right Relevance Influencers’ Topical Scores & Rankings

\r\n\r\n \t\t\t\t
\r\n\t\t\tPosted on January 27, 2017March 23, 2017 by Vishal Mishra\t\t\t\t\tLeave a comment\r\n\t\t\r\n\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\r\n\t\t\t\t blog-post, news\t\t\t\r\n\t\t\t\t\t\r\n\t\t\r\n\t\t
\r\n\t\t\t
\r\n\r\n\t
\r\n\t\t

Right Relevance (RR) provides curated information and intelligence on over 50 thousand topics. This includes:

\n
    \n
  • Topic relationships including related topics & semantic information like synonyms, acronyms.
    • \n
  • Topical influencers (over 2.5M) with score and rank.
    • \n
  • Topical content and information in the form of articles, videos and conversations.
    • \n
\n

Often asked questions are,\xc2\xa0what does score for a topic tag assigned to an influencer signify and how is it calculated. For e.g. the scores shown for ‘climate change‘ for the top 4 influencers, along with scores for the additional topic tags, in the image below.

\n\n

The Right Relevance score of an influencer for a TOPIC represents the authority within the social community for that topic, say for e.g. ‘machine learning’, of that influencer. It is a normalized score ranging from 10 to 100. This numeric influence is then inductively applied to the topical content curated by that individual for measuring relevance.

\n

The process is fully algorithmic and leverages\xc2\xa0ML, semantic analysis and NLP on unstructured data at scale. It is primarily graph based and involves performing a 2-level proprietary people rank (custom page rank for social graphs):

\n
\n

Stage 1. Global PR to reduce a ~300M nodes graph to ~6M (for now) globally ranked influencers. This is a first level reduction and we don’t expose the\xc2\xa0scores. It doesn’t have any topical context.

\n

Stage 2. Graph partitioning of the ~6M connected nodes\xc2\xa0from stage 1 across our ~50K structured topic space using unstructured data assigned to each\xc2\xa0node. This leads to ~50K\xc2\xa0per topic\xc2\xa0sub-graphs, where a secondary PR is applied to determine the topic score\xc2\xa0for\xc2\xa0each node in each topical sub-graph. This secondary PR score is normalized to calculate\xc2\xa0the Right Relevance topic score and rank\xc2\xa0influencers for every structured topic in our platform.\xc2\xa0

\n
\n

Our\xc2\xa0custom PR algorithm is derived from google pagerank but is specialized for social graphs (instead of links/webpages) with many important differences applicable to social networks.

\n

This\xc2\xa0measure of influence per topic is termed as\xc2\xa0\xe2\x80\x98topical influence\xe2\x80\x99 or Tribes. More details on our related Insights service including Tribes and Flocks is available here.

\n

Points to note:

\n
    \n
  • We dampen followers count, tweet count etc. noisy signals and lay much more focus on the topical network itself.
    • \n
  • Each influencer can be part of multiple topical sub-graphs aka communities and have a different score, and\xc2\xa0rank, within each. This is exposed in our apps via scored tags.
    • \n
  • Other, non structured, topics work via free-form search but the relevance may not be of the same quality. This can be seen by the score ’10’, which, probably poorly done, means we didn’t find a community for the topic.
    • \n
  • Both our topics and graph are mined and built algorithmically at scale and increasing in number with higher quality with every iteration.
    • \n
