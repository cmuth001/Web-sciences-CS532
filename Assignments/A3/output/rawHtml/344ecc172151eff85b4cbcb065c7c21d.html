Tottenham 2-0 Newport: Erik Lamela nets first goal in 504 days after Dan Butler\'s own goal as Mauricio Pochettino\'s side avoid upset to reach fifth round

\n
  • Mauricio Pochettino\'s Spurs earned their place in the fifth round with a 2-0 victory on Wednesday night
  • Tottenham took the lead after Moussa Sissoko\'s low cross was deflected into his own net by\xc2\xa0Dan Butler
  • Erik Lamela doubled the home side\'s advantage with his first goal for the club since September 2016\xc2\xa0
  • Awaiting\xc2\xa0the victorious Spurs in the next round is a trip to League One strugglers Rochdale on February 18

By\n\nMatt Barlow for the Daily Mail\n\n\n\n

Mauricio Pochettino claimed football was all about tricking your opponent as he tumbled over in defence of Dele Alli.

Danny Blanchflower said it was about \'glory\', \'doing things in style\' and with a \'flourish\' and Tottenham play a crackly old recording of the evocative words of their former captain before each home game.

Sometimes, though, it all seems so much simpler than that. Football, first and foremost, is about goals. And a bit of luck.\xc2\xa0

\n \n
\n\n \n

Tottenham cruised into the fifth round of the FA Cup thanks to a 2-0 victory over League Two side Newport at Wembley

\n \n
\n\n \n

The Premier League outfit took the lead after 26 minutes when the unfortunate Dan Butler turned a cross into his own net

\n \n
\n\n \n

On 34 minutes, Erik Lamela doubled the home side\'s advantage with his first goal for the club since September 2016

\n \n
\n\n \n

The Argentine forward fires the ball home beyond the stretching Scot Bennett and the reach of goalkeeper Joe Day

\n \n
\n\n \n

Tottenham took the lead after Dan Butler turned Moussa Sissoko\'s low cross into his own net. For more statistics and information see Sportsmail\'s brilliant MATCH ZONE.

Harry Kane grabbed a late equaliser in Newport last month to save Pochettino\'s team from suffering one of the FA Cup\'s greatest upsets in the mud of Rodney Parade.

And on Wednesday night they brushed the League Two side aside on the Wembley baize without the need for Kane to get off the bench.

Tottenham feasted on good fortune to take the lead when defender Dan Butler sliced into his own net.

Erik Lamela added the second before half-time with his first goal for 17 months.

There would have been more but for the extension of Fernando Llorente\'s personal finishing crisis but they move on to Rochdale in the last 16.

Just wait until Alli and Kane find Spurs are on their way to Spotland in the fifth round. It sounds like paradise for Tottenham\'s penalty twins.\xc2\xa0

Beat Rochdale on their notoriously poor pitch and they will be into the last 16 without encountering a team from any higher than the lower reaches of the third tier.\xc2\xa0

\n\n \n

England defender Danny Rose makes an acrobatic attempt to win the ball during the first half of Wednesday\'s encounter

\n \n
\n\n \n

Son Heung-min shoots for goal as Newport\'s David Pipe makes a diving attempted block during the FA Cup replay

\n \n
\n\n \n

Lamela tries to retain possession while under pressure from Newport\'s defensive midfielder Ben Tozer during the first half

\n \n
\n\n \n

Newport\'s goal hero from the two sides\' first meeting, Padraig Amond, battles for a high ball with Tottenham\'s Juan Foyth

Win two more ties and they will be into the semi-final and back at Wembley where they have been lodging all season and are now unbeaten in a dozen games and face Arsenal on Saturday.

Sometimes things can fall into place in the knock-out competitions. Tottenham will hope this is the year it does in a competition they last won it in 1991.

Pochettino made 10 changes in the hope of keeping some first-teamers fresh for the North London derby.

Only Heung-min Son survived to the team which drew at Liverpool, and there were welcome returns made by three of Pochettino\'s key men.

Toby Alderweireld made his first appearance since suffering a hamstring injury against Real Madrid on November 1.\xc2\xa0Danny Rose and Harry Winks were both in action for the first time since Boxing Day.

Rose, back from a knee injury, slammed the first chance of the game into the side-netting from a cross by Moussa Sissoko.\xc2\xa0

Newport had lost both their League Two games since holding Spurs in south Wales but opened with the same confidence and energy which has defined their transformation under Flynn.\xc2\xa0

\n\n \n

Victor Wanyama, who netted a stunner in Spurs\' 2-2 draw at Liverpool last weekend, went into the book early in the game

\n \n
\n\n \n

Pochettino rested a number of players for the match, with Harry Kane and Christian Eriksen among those on the bench

\n \n
\n\n \n

English midfielder Harry Winks, who has been plagued with an ankle problem since Christmas, turns with the ball at his feet

\n \n
\n\n \n

The 22-year-old central midfielder unleashes a shot at goal but Tottenham had to wait for a defensive error to take the lead

They were heading out of the Football League when he took over last year and probably into liquidation had they been relegated but here they were ruffling Premier League feathers at Wembley.

Spurs were hustled into mistakes in the opening phase of the game and were bombarded by Ben Tozer\'s exceptionally long throw.

Victor Wanyama was booked for a foul on Joss Labadie after being played into trouble by young central defender Juan Foyth.

Gradually, however, Tottenham seized control, began to dominate in midfield and went ahead with a slice of fortune in the 26th minute.

Llorente held up the ball on the edge of the penalty box and worked it out to the right.

Sissoko\'s early cross caught out Butler. As the left back tried to adjust his feet and side-foot clear, the ball clipped the inside of his right leg and flashed past his own goalkeeper Joe Day at the near post.\xc2\xa0

\'Not many can say they scored at Wembley, but Dan Butler can,\' smiled manager Flynn after the match, determined to savour the occasion if not the result, but was a cruel way to slip behind.

The goal served to help Spurs relax into a better attacking flow. Day made saves to deny Lamela and Son and the keeper was soon beaten again.\xc2\xa0

\n \n
\n\n \n

Lamela tries to avoid the attention of two Newport players as Spurs cruised into the fifth round with victory on Wednesday

Son found Lamela who applied a sweet touch on the run and a tidy finish and sprinted away to celebrate his first goal since September 2016, when he was among the scorers in a League Cup tie against Gillingham.

Minds flashed to Notts County\'s eight-goal drubbing at the hands of Swansea in a fourth round replay on the previous night and yet Newport, to their immense credit, regrouped and refused to buckle.

In the second half, they dug in to frustrate their illustrious hosts even when legs were weary and Christian Eriksen and Alli came off the bench.

Day saved again from Lamela, Eriksen fired wide, Alli hit the bar when clean through and Llorente missed a couple of glorious openings, one a header and one a volley, both inches wide.

On the break, Newport made occasional forays to bring their following of more than 7,000 to its feet and Padraig Amond, scorer in the initial tie, forced a save from Michel Vorm in the closing seconds.

\'I\'m a bit disappointed we didn\'t score at the end,\' said Flynn. \'The players deserved it and the fans would have made a right noise.\'

Newport, however, can take inspiration from their brush with the Premier League as they return to the toil of League Two and a fixture against Forest Green Rovers on Saturday.

Tottenham move on to Arsenal and Juventus. Then Rochdale.\xc2\xa0
