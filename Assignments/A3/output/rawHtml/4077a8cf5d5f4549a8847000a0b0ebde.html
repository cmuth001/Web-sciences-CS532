Nazim Derry and Ahmad Jones took the next step in their football and academic careers Wednesday afternoon.

The Atlantic City High School seniors both signed National Letters of Intent to play football at NCAA Division II colleges on a scholarship.

Derry will attend Virginia Union University, while Jones is headed to Shepherd University in Shepherdstown, West Virginia.

\xe2\x80\x9cIt\xe2\x80\x99s a blessing,\xe2\x80\x9d Derry said. \xe2\x80\x9cI feel like it should be motivation for younger kids. I feel like the younger kids are going to see me do it, so they\xe2\x80\x99re going to feel like they can do it. I\xe2\x80\x99m just blessed.\xe2\x80\x9d

Wednesday marked the start of the second college National Letter of Intent signing period for high school football players. The days used to make much more news in the past, but this fall the NCAA started an early-signing period in December for football players. Many top players signed during that period.

Atlantic City finished 6-4 last fall, ending the season with a 36-32 win over rival Holy Spirit. Vikings wide receiver Nah\xe2\x80\x99Sir Morgan signed in December to attend Division I University of Maine.

\xe2\x80\x9cIt means the world to me and the coaches,\xe2\x80\x9d Atlantic City coach Thomas Kelly said of his players going to college. \xe2\x80\x9cThis is what we signed up for. Wins and losses are important, but these guys going to college is what it\xe2\x80\x99s all about.\xe2\x80\x9d

Derry excelled at quarterback, running back, wide receiver and defensive back for the Vikings. He couldn\xe2\x80\x99t have had a better tour guide when he visited Virginia Union last month. Pete Hunter, a 1996 Atlantic City graduate and Virginia Union All-American, flew in from Texas to show him around. Hunter played five years as defensive back in the NFL, mostly with the Dallas Cowboys.

\xe2\x80\x9cPete was a big part of my decision,\xe2\x80\x9d Derry said. \xe2\x80\x9cMe and him are really close. I talk to him all the time.\xe2\x80\x9d

Derry caught 32 passes for 489 yards last fall. He also threw for 738 yards and ran for 301 yards. Derry scored 11 touchdowns.

Virginia Union finished 6-4 last season. Derry said the Virginia Union coaches see him as an offensive player, most likely wide receiver.

\n
\n \n
\n

\xe2\x80\x9cThey made me feel at home,\xe2\x80\x9d Derry said of the Virginia Union coaches. \xe2\x80\x9cThe campus is not big, so you know everyone there.\xe2\x80\x9d

Jones starred at linebacker for the Vikings. He will play a combination of linebacker and defensive back for Shepherd. The Rams finished 10-1 last season. Running back Ifreke Andy, a 2017 Absegami graduate, was a redshirt freshman at Shepherd last fall.

\xe2\x80\x9cWhat I really liked about Shepherd was the football tradition,\xe2\x80\x9d Jones said. \xe2\x80\x9cThe football tradition got me on board.\xe2\x80\x9d

Jones said he plans to major in business.

\xe2\x80\x9cThis is really exciting,\xe2\x80\x9d Jones said. \xe2\x80\x9cGrowing up ever since I was young I always wanted to play college football. Now, getting this scholarship to Shepherd is taking me one step closer to my dream.\xe2\x80\x9d
