Former St Mirren chairman Stewart Gilmour urges SFA to bring Brian Caldwell in to replace Stewart Regan as chief executive

\n\n\t\t
\n\t\t

The Scotland chiefs are searching for a replacement on the board and the dugout and the Shrewsbury Town man is on the list to take over from Regan

\n\t
\n\t
\n\n
\n\n\t\t\t
\n\t\t\tExclusive\n\t\t
\n\t\n\t
\n\t\t\t\t\t
\n\t\t\t\t\n\t\t\t\t\tBy Roger Hannah\t\t\t\t\n\t\t\t
\n\t\t\n\t\t
\n\t\t\t
\n\t\t\t\t5th February 2018,\n\t\t\t\t10:30 pm\n\t\t\t
\n\n\t\t\t\t
\n\n\t
\n
\n\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t
\n\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t

STEWART GILMOUR last night urged SFA chiefs to bring Brian Caldwell back to Scotland as their new chief executive.

\n

Shrewsbury Town supremo Caldwell, 49, has emerged as a top candidate since Stewart Regan was axed last Thursday.

\n\n\n

SunSport understands the former St Mirren and Ayr United chief fits the criteria set by members of the seven-man selection panel.

\n

And former Buddies chairman Gilmour \xe2\x80\x94 a vocal opponent of Regan during his eight-year reign \xe2\x80\x94 believes Caldwell would be the perfect man for the job.

\n

He told SunSport: \xe2\x80\x9cBrian\xe2\x80\x99s experience is beyond any other candidate who comes to mind. It would be of huge benefit to the SFA to bring in someone who has run a football club or, in Brian\xe2\x80\x99s case, THREE.

\n

\xe2\x80\x9cHe\xe2\x80\x99d know everything he needs to know to fulfil that role. He has worked from League Two through to the Premiership and now has experience of working in the EFL too.

\n

\xe2\x80\x9cI\xe2\x80\x99m sure there will be other candidates, but he would be my man for the job.\xe2\x80\x9d

\n

Caldwell entered football administration as a 25-year-old under Bill Barr at Somerset Park and was involved during Ayr\xe2\x80\x99s Hampden visits with Gordon Dalziel.

\n\n\n

He was headhunted by St Mirren to preside over the new era when they moved stadia and played a role in their League Cup Final success with Danny Lennon.

\n

He has helped haul Shrewsbury from rock bottom of SkyBet League One to second place in just over a year, having targeted Paul Hurst as their new boss.

\n

It is the kind of CV which Gilmour insists must not be ignored by the Hampden hierarchy as they consider Regan\xe2\x80\x99s replacement. He said: \xe2\x80\x9cWhen we spoke to Brian about the role at St Mirren, he\xe2\x80\x99d actually left day-to-day work at Ayr and was back in one of Bill\xe2\x80\x99s companies as financial controller.

\n

\xe2\x80\x9cWe had gutted St Mirren and needed someone to come in and rebuild the club.

\n

\xe2\x80\x9cWe knew straight away he was the best man for the job and didn\xe2\x80\x99t even interview anyone else. I suppose you could say he was headhunted.

\n

\xe2\x80\x9cHe did a fantastic job, particularly the way he liaised with all parts of the club. He could pull the staff together, pull the fans together and even had an ability to handle the directors and chairman!

\n

\xe2\x80\x9cA lot of the job of a chief executive is man management of people, as well as giving the club or business a direction.

\n\n\n

\xe2\x80\x9cBrian made all the employees from top to bottom feel that they were all part of it. He could speak to people in their language.

\n

\xe2\x80\x9cWe were still at Love Street when he arrived and he played a big part in the move. The construction of the dome at the new stadium was completely his baby.

\n

\xe2\x80\x9cHis contacts and his ability to attract players were superb, too. Gary Teale would never have been at St Mirren if it wasn\xe2\x80\x99t for Brian, who\xe2\x80\x99d worked with him at Ayr.

\n

\xe2\x80\x9cHe knew Danny as well and he had full input in everything that would happen at the club.

\n

\n

\xe2\x80\x9cHe could also counter the board if he felt that we were going down the wrong avenue.

\n

\xe2\x80\x9cHe is now doing a tremendous job at Shrewsbury.\xe2\x80\x9d

\n

Gilmour feels the job specification of SFA chief executive should be changed.

\n

He added: \xe2\x80\x9cI met Gordon Smith last week and we had a chat about the role of SFA chief executive.
