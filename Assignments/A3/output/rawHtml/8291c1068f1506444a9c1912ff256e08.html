\nTrain in the Off Season to Get Better!\n

\n
\n
\n
\n
\n
\nAdaptation is a process that takes place during recovery not during training. Additionally, rest, food, hydration and emotional environment define recovery. 
\n
\n
\n
\nAsk any great athlete and they will tell you exactly how important their off-season is to their in-season performance. Great football players are notorious for their off-season workouts. Specialty training for football is tricky if you\'re a young athlete who participates in other sports during the off-season. Multi-sport participation is encouraged at the young ages. You must be cautious to implement off-season football training carefully if you\'re doing another sport at the same time. Players should take a few weeks off after every season to do nothing before slowly getting back into into training. When they do, they start from scratch with light activities designed to reset their connective tissue and neuromuscular system to a baseline that allows them to build a foundation of strength, durability and explosive power just in time for preseason. It\'s also great to stretch, do yoga and relax the body after months of competition. Training in the off-season is important to development and injury prevention.

\nOff-Season Exercises\n

\n
\n
\n
\n
\n
\nThe off-season for many youth football organizations is here. A season of training, drills and workouts comes to a grinding halt for many athletes, though it really does not have to be over. Physical training year-round keeps kids along that disciplined and structured route, and we encourage the consistency.  Many kids transition into other sports, such as basketball, which is also a great way to keep to train for football. It’s all about consistency and keeping busy. The alternative could be laziness, and this is not beneficial to your athlete.
\n
\n
\n
\nHere are 3 essential exercizes to incorporate into your child’s off-season schedule:
\n
\n
\n
\n1. The push-up. No weights needed for this, what is widely considered the ultimate strength training exercise. 4 sets of 7 reps.
\n
\n
\n
\n2. The air squat for leg strength and power.  Squats are important for building leg muscle strength, this exercise, as the name implies, does not need weights. Just bring in determination. 4 sets of 5-10 repetitions is ideal, vary the amount of reps just to switch it up.
\n
\n
\n\n\n
\n
\n
\n
\n3. Jump Rope.  Fun! Portable, cheap and a fundamental exercise. It’s not just about cardio. Strength training for hamstrings, thighs, calves and even arms. Jumping rope, with the right technique is low impact with high benefit. Balance and coordination improves greatly as well. 4 sets.
