    Placing a call to the Graves\xe2\x80\x99 family home in Gibsonville on Tuesday the excitement I heard in their voices was evident.

    Dominique Graves, a 6-2 left-handed quarterback from Eastern Guilford, and his twin brother, Kendyl, are two of the incoming recruits Coach Kienus Boulware and his staff have landed. The class will be finalized sometime later this week and I\'ll have a full story on the signings for Friday\'s newspaper.

    Teressa Graves, who is Dominque and Kendyl\xe2\x80\x99s mother, says she\xe2\x80\x99s excited that they are going to the same college.

    \xe2\x80\x9cIt\xe2\x80\x99s great that they can spend four more years together,\xe2\x80\x9d Teressa said. \xe2\x80\x9cThey have a great relationship and are very competitive with each other and that includes academically.\xe2\x80\x9d

    The twins helped Eastern Guilford to a 12-3 record as they made it to the state semifinals. And they are also very good in the classroom sporting 3.5 grade point averages.

    Dominique was the Greensboro News & Record player of the year this past season.

    He passed for 2,072 yards completing 61 percent of his passes for 18 touchdowns and seven interceptions. He was also a very good runner going for 1,248 yards on 223 carries with 23 touchdowns.

    Coach Doug Robertson of Eastern Guilford said earlier this year that Dominique has also excelled in the classroom.

    \xe2\x80\x9cDominique is a dynamic athlete,\xe2\x80\x9d Robertson said. \xe2\x80\x9cHe is an even better person. He is a leader for our football team and for our school. He not only won many athletics awards, but also won an academic award for the highest GPA on the team.\xe2\x80\x9d

    Dominique, who is also a starter on Eastern Guilford\xe2\x80\x99s basketball team, says one reason he chose WSSU was because the coaches were on him early.

    \xe2\x80\x9cWSSU was the only school that offered me a scholarship,\xe2\x80\x9d Dominique said. \xe2\x80\x9cThey were the first ones to do so and I jumped at the chance. I\xe2\x80\x99m excited about it and can\xe2\x80\x99t wait to get there later this summer.\xe2\x80\x9d

    Rod Tinsley, a three-year starter, will be the incumbent heading into the fall but Graves says he hopes to get a shot at playing.

    \xe2\x80\x9cThe coaches indicated that I won\xe2\x80\x99t be redshirted and will get a chance to compete for the starting job,\xe2\x80\x9d Graves said.

    The Rams are expected to sign about 20 players for this coming fall. Among those who will sign two will likely be transfers but the rest of them are incoming high school players.
