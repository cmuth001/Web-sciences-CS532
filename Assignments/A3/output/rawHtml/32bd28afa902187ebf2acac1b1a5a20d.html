Openly gay high school football star honored by St. Louis Blues on pride night

\n\n \n
\n
\n \n \n New,\n \n \n \n 1\n \n comment\n \n
\n\n
\n \n

Jake Bain has become a role model since coming out.

When St. Louis high school football star Jake Bain came out openly as gay in December, he never thought that less than two months later he would be honored by the NHL\xe2\x80\x99s St. Louis Blues as their hometown hero.

\n
\n
\n

I am incredibly honored to be the Blues\xe2\x80\x99 hometown hero tonight at Pride Night \xef\xb8\x8f\xe2\x80\x8d The Blues continue to set the bar for the rest of professional sports teams to follow in showing support for the LGBT community. I am proud to be a Blues fan. #stlblues #LGB pic.twitter.com/0dUfIj6v2g

\xe2\x80\x94 Jake Bain (@Jakebain10) February 7, 2018\n
\n\n
\n

The Blues honored Bain as part of the team\xe2\x80\x99s LGBT pride night celebration Tuesday.

\n

\xe2\x80\x9cAnyone can be gay and the fact that the Blues are being able to take this opportunity to show that is a really special moment,\xe2\x80\x9d he told a local TV station.

\n

Bain was Missouri\xe2\x80\x99s Class 3 Offensive Player of the Year as a sophomore, state champ as a junior and team captain as a senior. This fall he will play football for Indiana State University, a Division I school.

\n

Since coming out, Bain has spoken at various events about his struggles of coming out, the acceptance he has found and the encouragement he gives other LGBT people who are facing a similar dilemma. It\xe2\x80\x99s awesome that the Blues recognized the difference he is making.

\n

\xe2\x80\x9cMy goal has always been to show the generation of kids that come after me that it\xe2\x80\x99s OK to be whoever you want to be\xe2\x80\x9d he said.
