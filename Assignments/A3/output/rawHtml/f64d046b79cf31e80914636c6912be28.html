HAMMOND -- Frank Scelfo\xe2\x80\x99s first signing class features two from Livingston Parish, and he\xe2\x80\x99s hoping that\xe2\x80\x99s just the start of things for athletes in the area when it comes to playing at Southeastern.

The Lions added seven players Wednesday, including Walker linebacker Josh Cook and kicker Nathan Holliday of Live Oak.

The players the Lions added Wednesday are in addition to 10 players who signed during the early signing period.

\n
\n\n
\n\n\n
\n
\n

Scelfo said getting Cook into the fold at SLU was helped by the fact that he has ties to Walker coach Lester Ricard, having coached Ricard at Tulane. Holliday was coached by Brett Beard, who played for the Lions in 2003.

\xe2\x80\x9cI\xe2\x80\x99m going to Walker High School,\xe2\x80\x9d Scelfo said. \xe2\x80\x9cI\xe2\x80\x99m going to see Lester. I\xe2\x80\x99m close to his mom, his dad, his wife. Not only this year, but in the future, we\xe2\x80\x99re going to camp out there. We\xe2\x80\x99re going to that school, and we\xe2\x80\x99re going to get every kid that we can out of that school that we feel can help us win. The same thing with Brett.

\xe2\x80\x9cThe thing with Lester, his loyalty would be to me. Brett\xe2\x80\x99s is the other way. He loves Hammond and Southeastern Louisiana. He just met me, so it\xe2\x80\x99s my job to build that trust with him to know that we\xe2\x80\x99re going to do everything that we can with his guys to help them out.\xe2\x80\x9d

Scelfo said he watched Cook, who had over 100 tackles last season, play basketball for the Wildcats and was impressed. Cook was twice voted to the All-Parish football team, this season as the Co-Defensive MVP.

\xe2\x80\x9cThe guy is player, man,\xe2\x80\x9d Scelfo said. \xe2\x80\x9cReally unique guy. He\xe2\x80\x99s a good player.\xe2\x80\x9d

Sclefo said he visited with Beard and Holliday and he\xe2\x80\x99s hoping to grow the relationship with Live Oak.

\xe2\x80\x9c(Beard) is a good guy,\xe2\x80\x9d Scelfo said. \xe2\x80\x9cHe\xe2\x80\x99s going to always have players there. We want those players to come here. We want to keep them here.

\xe2\x80\x9c(Holliday\xe2\x80\x99s) a local guy right here in our own backyard, so we didn\xe2\x80\x99t want to lose him. He\xe2\x80\x99s got a strong leg.\xe2\x80\x9d

The majority of the Lions\xe2\x80\x99 first signing class was already in the fold, with nine players signing in December and two Division I transfers -- defensive back Xavier Lewis (LSU) and quarterback Chason Virgil (Fresno State -- joining the program in January.

\xe2\x80\x9cThe staff did a really good job identifying need positions, so they went after those guys,\xe2\x80\x9d said Scelfo, who was introduced as the Lions\xe2\x80\x99 coach last week.

\xe2\x80\x9cReally pleased with the guys \xe2\x80\x93 not only the talent level that we got, but the numbers to fill in some of those positions due to graduation and things like that.\xe2\x80\x9d

The class features four linebackers and three defensive backs. At linebacker, in addition to Cook, the Lions added Josh Carr of Houston, Mike Mason of El Camino College and Semaj Thomas of Jones County JC. The defensive backs are Lewis, Matthew Wright of Navarre College and Donniel Ward-Magee of St. Augustine HS.

\xe2\x80\x9cThere\xe2\x80\x99s a big loss on the back end, the back seven, with a lot of guys this past season, so there will be a lot of new faces there this coming year,\xe2\x80\x9d Scelfo said.

\xe2\x80\x9cWe feel like those guys will be able to step in and contribute immediately. We need them to.\xe2\x80\x9d

The Lions also added four offensive linemen \xe2\x80\x93 Jarius Gooch of Holmes Community College, Kaleb Hogg of Sulphur High, Brendon Kelley of Pine and Brennan Lanclos of Lakeshore High.

Running back Taron Jones (Mandeville HS), defensive lineman Dante Carter (St. Augustine HS), receiver Marquis Williams (Palomar College) and quarterback Chris Penton (Lakeshore HS) round out the class.

Scelfo said he\xe2\x80\x99s counting on the Lions\xe2\x80\x99 junior college signees to make an immediate impact.

\xe2\x80\x9cThe junior college guys, the transfers, these are not developmental guys,\xe2\x80\x9d he said. \xe2\x80\x9cThese guys are coming in to compete right now for the job.\xe2\x80\x9d

Although the Lions already have quarterbacks Lorenzo Nunez and Byron Walker in the fold from last season, Scelfo isn\xe2\x80\x99t concerned about adding Virgil and Penton.

\xe2\x80\x9cI think what you\xe2\x80\x99ve got to be able to do is you\xe2\x80\x99ve got to manage the room, and then you create competition,\xe2\x80\x9d Scelfo said of the quarterback situation.

\xe2\x80\x9cCan you have too many? Nope. You need as many as you can get \xe2\x80\x93 create competition.\xe2\x80\x9d
