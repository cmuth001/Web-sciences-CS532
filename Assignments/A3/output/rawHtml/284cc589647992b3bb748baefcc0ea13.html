Syracuse, N.Y. -- With national signing day coming to a close and Syracuse football\'s 2018 recruiting more or less sewn up, it\'s time to see how the Orange fares against its ACC competition.

\n

There hasn\'t been a ton of movement in the ACC rankings since we last posted after the early signing period in December. Back then, the rankings looked as follows:

1. Miami (Fla.)

\n

2. Clemson

\n

3. Virginia Tech

\n

4. North Carolina State

\n

13. Duke

Dabo Swinney has built a force in Clemson, and with a stocked roster set to return for another national title run in 2018, Clemson is showing no signs of slowing down on the recruiting trail.

\n

National rank: 5

\n

5 stars: 5

\n

4 star: 7

\n

3 stars: 5

Miami returned to national relevance in 2017, and Mark Richt capitalized on being located in perhaps the most advantageous geographic region in the conference.

\n

\n

5 stars: 1

\n

4 stars: 14

\n

\n

Who really thought Florida State was going to hover in the middle of the pack in the ACC? The infrastructure and access to good recruits make this a job that is hard to botch.

\n

Willie Taggart made inroads in talent-rich Florida back during his days as head coach at South Florida. Now he has control at one of the state\'s three flagship programs.

5 stars: 0

Head coach Justin Fuente last week agreed to a new contract that also increased the salary pool for his staff, the latest sign of the school\'s commitment to a coach that has popped up on athletic directors\' lists for head-coaching openings.
