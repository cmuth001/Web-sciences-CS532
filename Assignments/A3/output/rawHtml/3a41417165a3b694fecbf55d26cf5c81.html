Kansas University football recruiting\n

\n

National Signing Day has arrived for the Class of 2018, but Kansas football coach David Beaty and his staff don\xe2\x80\x99t have to worry much about in terms of which of their recruiting targets will follow through and sign letters of intent.

\n

The time for heightened anxiousness came and went in December\xe2\x80\x99s three-day early signing period, when Louisiana standouts Corione Harris and Anthony \xe2\x80\x9cPooka\xe2\x80\x9d Williams joined three other preps and nine junior college prospects in making their commitments official.

\n

Throw in Florida State graduate transfer Mavin Saunders and juco quarterback Miles Kendrick, who joined the program for the spring semester, and the KU staff entered the traditional February signing day with most of its business behind them.

\n

Only four players who had previously announced their commitments to Kansas came into Wednesday unsigned: Blue Valley North defensive end Miles Emery, Louisiana linebacker Ryan Malbrough, Texas prep quarterback Torry Locklin and Coffeyville Community College offensive tackle Reuben Lewis.

\n

That group gained a welcome addition Wednesday morning, though, when Kenny Bastida, an uncommitted three-star linebacker from Deerfield Beach, Fla., decided he would sign with Kansas.

\n

According to Jon Kirby of Jayhawk Slant, Bastida visited KU this past weekend after being recruited by new linebackers coach Bill Miller. Prior to his senior season, Bastida reportedly had offers from Penn State, LSU, Michigan State, Wisconsin, North Carolina State and several other programs. He told Kirby in the past month he visited with coaches from Missouri, North Carolina, South Carolina, Colorado and \xe2\x80\x9ca lot of other schools.\xe2\x80\x9d

\n

A 6-foot-4, 330-pound tackle originally from Apopka, Fla., Lewis was the first of the group to sign Wednesday morning.

\n

The letter for Malbrough, a 6-1, 180-pound linebacker from Cecilia, La., came through next.

\n

Bastida signed Wednesday morning, as well.
