Rangers chiefs hail new funding deal and rubbish claims Ibrox club is cash-strapped

The Ibrox board have been granted an overdraft of \xe2\x80\x9cmodest millions\xe2\x80\x9d with Close Brothers to fund a range of projects at the club.

Rangers have agreed a funding facility with a leading merchant banker and claim it\xe2\x80\x99s another step towards financial recovery.

The Ibrox board have been granted an overdraft of \xe2\x80\x9cmodest millions\xe2\x80\x9d with Close Brothers to fund a range of projects at the club.

Record Sport understands the facility is less than £3 million, but managing director Stewart Robertson insists there is no danger of chairman Dave King and his fellow investors withdrawing their backing.

He has also refuted suggestions the club, who have relied on soft loans from King and Co in recent years, are cash-strapped.

Rather, Robertson says it\xe2\x80\x99s evidence of the credibility of the current board among City moneymen as Rangers prepare to launch a share issue later in the year.

Robertson said: \xe2\x80\x9cIt\xe2\x80\x99s another positive step in the club moving forward. We turn over more than £30 million a year and there are very few businesses with that level of turnover that don\xe2\x80\x99t have a banking facility.

\xe2\x80\x9cSome folk take pleasure in winding us up because we don\xe2\x80\x99t have a bank facility but this is the next stage in moving the club forward and getting it onto a sensible business footing.

\xe2\x80\x9cAs trite as it sounds, the money is almost incidental. it\xe2\x80\x99s about someone having belief in the club and the way it is being run as we move forward and normalise operations.

\xe2\x80\x9cDave and the other investors remain firmly behind the club. The contingency is still there if we need money for a particular project or to head into the transfer market in the summer.

\xe2\x80\x9cWe need to get to the position we\xe2\x80\x99re a self sustaining football club. That has always been the target. We can\xe2\x80\x99t keep going back to the well.

\xe2\x80\x9cA £30 million business with no bank facilities is very, very unusual. It\xe2\x80\x99s another step and it\xe2\x80\x99s about doing it within the realms of financial prudence.

\xe2\x80\x9cWe\xe2\x80\x99re not going daft with it, we\xe2\x80\x99re doing it within the framework of what the business can afford and it involves modest millions, which are sustainable and serviceable.

\xe2\x80\x9cWe must operate at a sensible level and the banks aren\xe2\x80\x99t going to throw silly sums of money at clubs going forward. There is a check and balance on the whole thing.\xe2\x80\x9d

The overdraft will be secured against Edmiston House and the Albion car park and is in addition to the £7 million King promised auditors he\xe2\x80\x99d underwrite for any losses in the current financial year.

The facility will be used to bolster the club\xe2\x80\x99s finances at a time of the year when clubs are traditionally stretched and it also comes ahead of the start next week of a £3 million refurb of the Ibrox fabric.

Robertson added: \xe2\x80\x9cIt\xe2\x80\x99s general working capital, because finances are stronger in the summer when season ticket and SPFL money comes in.

\xe2\x80\x9cThere\'s lots of work going on around the stadium, which lifts the morale of people who work here, as well as the supporters who can see what\xe2\x80\x99s going on.

\xe2\x80\x9cWe\xe2\x80\x99ve put out all the fires and now we can put the foundations down to take Ibrox back to where it should be as a stadium.

\xe2\x80\x9cClose Brothers would not have come to us if they thought there were still issues with the club in any shape or form so it\xe2\x80\x99s a vote of confidence in the way Rangers are moving forward.

\xe2\x80\x9cThey can see the investment already made and there\xe2\x80\x99s a share issue coming down the road as well. Step by step we\xe2\x80\x99re beginning to make progress.\xe2\x80\x9d

King is currently in talks with the Takeover Panel after they ordered him to make an offer of 20 pence a share after he was found to have acted in concert with the Three Bears when seizing control of the club three years ago.

King appealed the decision by the Court of Session and that issue needs to be resolved before Rangers can offer a new shares issue.

Rangers knocked back a bid from Chinese club Beijing Renhe for Alfredo Morelos last week that could have netted them up to £7.5 million.

Robertson said: \xe2\x80\x9cThere\xe2\x80\x99s still a way to go but we\xe2\x80\x99re getting into a position where we\xe2\x80\x99re much stronger than we were. We\xe2\x80\x99re trying to build a squad rather than take it apart.\xe2\x80\x9d
