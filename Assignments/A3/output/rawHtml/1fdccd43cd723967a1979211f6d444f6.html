\tBy Chetan Narula

Cape Town, Feb 7 (PTI) Virat Kohli\'s masterclass and a\rpouncing pack of wrist-spinners ensured a 124-run win for\rIndia in the third ODI here, giving the visitors an\runassailable 3-0 lead and taking them one step closer to a\rmaiden series triumph in South Africa.

Kohli reaffirmed his status as an ODI great with an\runbeaten 160 off 159 balls on a challenging surface,\rpropelling India to 303 for six.

\n\n\n
\n

It was the 34th ODI hundred for the Indian captain, who\ris now 15 shy of Sachin Tendulkar\'s mark of 49.

Kohli has been simply unstoppable in the 50-over format\rof late and today\'s match-winning effort was his fourth\rthree-figure score in the last six ODIs. It was also his\rsecond hundred of the series and third of the tour.

While most Indian batsmen struggled after South Africa\ropted to bowl, Kohli made batting look easy. Shikhar Dhawan\rtoo stood out with a fluent 76 off 63 balls.

South Africa started the chase on a promising note with\rcaptain Aiden Markram (32) and JP Duminy (51) sharing a 78-run\rstand for the second wicket.

But most of their runs came against the pacers and when\rIndian spinners got into the act, it was a familiar story as\rSouth Africa surrendered to Yuzvendra Chahal (4/46) and\rKuldeep Yadav (4/23) to end way behind at 170 all out in 40\rovers.

\n\n\n
\n

The South African batsmen failed to pick India\'s wily\rwrist-spinners, as was the case in the first two ODIs.

The slide began with the fall of Markram, who was stumped\rby MS Dhoni after getting beaten by a flighted delivery from\rYadav.

It happened to be Dhoni\'s 400th dismissal in ODIs as he\rbecame only the fourth wicket-keeper to reach that milestone\rin ODIs.

Not reading the spinners led to South African batsmen\rbeing defensive which pushed the run-rate up. From a healthy\r79 for one in the 17th over, the hosts were reduced to 129 for\rfive in the 33rd over, ending all their hopes of a recovery.

Earlier, Kohli scored the highest individual score by an\rIndian batsman against South Africa in South Africa, going\rpast Sourav Gangulys 127 at Johannesburg in 2001. It was also\rKohlis second-highest ODI score after 183 against Pakistan in\r2012.

Such was the nature of his innings that he scored only 60\rruns through boundaries while he had to run hard for the\rremaining 100. In total, he collected 12 fours and two sixes.

He also became the first Indian batsman to score a\rhundred against South Africa at Newlands. Only Ganguly\rpreviously had an ODI century at this ground, but that came\ragainst Kenya in March 2003.

Records kept tumbling as his innings progressed. He also\rbecame the Indian ODI captain with most hundreds to his name.

He went past Ganguly again, who has 11 centuries in 142\rinnings. Kohli now boasts of 12 hundreds as skipper but in\ronly 43 innings.

Kohli, with 318 runs in this series so far, now also has\rthe most runs for a visiting batsman in a bilateral ODI series\ragainst South Africa in South Africa. Ricky Ponting, with 283\rruns in 2001-02, was the previous best.

In terms of balls faced, it was the joint-slowest hundred\rfor Kohli, after he faced 119 balls against Pakistan at\rAdelaide in 2015. But in doing so, Kohli put on 140 runs with\rDhawan for the second wicket.

The Proteas made three changes to their playing eleven\rafter the Centurion defeat. Lungi Ngidi and Heinrich Klaasen\rwere handed their ODI debuts and came in for Morne Morkel and\rinjured Quinton de Kock, respectively.

Andile Phehlukwayo was included ahead of Tabraiz Shamsi\ras the hosts played only full-time spinner. India fielded an\runchanged side again.

Rohit Sharma (0) continued his poor run in this ODI\rseries and was caught behind off the sixth ball he faced. The\rthree-time ODI double-centurion now averages 12.10 in 10\rinnings on South African soil and has an ODI high score of\ronly 23.

Kohli (on 0*) survived an early lbw shout off Kagiso\rRabada (1-54) in the third over. He immediately asked for DRS\rreview, which showed an inside edge and the decision was\roverturned.

Dhawan and Kohli then came together, and piled on the\rruns, almost continuing from where they had left off in the\rprevious match.

Momentarily though, Indias run-rate dipped below 5/over\ras Chris Morris (1-45) exerted some control over proceedings.\rIn the meantime, Dhawan scored his 25th ODI half-century off\r42 balls and took his team past 100 in the 18th over.

Dhawan accelerated after reaching his mark, and scored 26\rruns off next 21 balls he faced. Part of the reason had to do\rwith Imran Tahirs (1-52) inclusion into the attack as\rDhawan-Kohli pairing milked his bowling for runs.

JP Duminy (2-60) then provided the breakthrough. In fact,\rit was stand-in skipper Markram who did all the hard work and\rtook a stunning diving catch to dismiss Dhawan who had danced\rdown to the part-timer.

South Africa then triggered a mini-collapse in the middle\rorder as India lost three wickets for 48 runs.

MS Dhoni (10) then stemmed the down-curve and added 40\rruns with Kohli.

Kedar Jadhav (1) played a nothing shot and edged behind\roff Phehlukwayo (1-42) as India were reduced to 236 for six in\rthe 43rd over.

The skipper then found a stable partner in Bhuvneshwar\rKumar (16 not out) and the duo added 63 runs off only 43 balls\rfor the seventh wicket
