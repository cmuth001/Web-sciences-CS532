Liverpool star Mohamed Salah on his Champions League memories, supporting the Reds and knowing he's good

Reds favourite says he grew up dreaming of playing in Europe's premier competition

  • by Andy Kelly
  • 15:10, 15 Feb 2018
\n
\n \n

If Carlsberg did interviews this was the one Liverpool fans around the world wanted to hear.

\n

Mohamed Salah , the new King of the Kop, speaking about his love for the club and how he is now playing where he has always wanted to play.

\n
\n \n
\n

With 28 goals under his belt already - including two against Spurs on Sunday - Salah\xe2\x80\x99s stunning impact at Anfield has had the unwanted knock-on effect of starting suggestions that Salah would be a target for a move away from Anfield, perhaps as early as this summer.

\n

But in his first major magazine interview as a Red, Salah has told FourFourTwo that he\xe2\x80\x99s loving life at LFC.

\n
\n \n
\n

Salah told their March 2018 edition: \xe2\x80\x9cI have loved the club since I was young and knew this was a team I wanted to play for.

\n

\xe2\x80\x9cI knew the history this club has and, as soon as I got the chance, I had to make it happen.\xe2\x80\x9d

\n\n \n

Such protestations have been heard from new players before of course and some observers might wonder why such fondness didn\xe2\x80\x99t see Salah arrive on Merseyside in January 2014 instead of Chelsea?

\n

Maybe that explains why his representatives then are no longer in place and he is now looked after by his new agent Ramy Abbas.

\n
\n \n
\n

Salah speaks warmly of his relationship with Jurgen Klopp and his teammates - especially Sadio Mane - at Anfield, while also insisting he continues to have a good relationship with Jose Mourinho despite his issues at Chelsea. He also looks back on his childhood where he started playing - as a left-back!
