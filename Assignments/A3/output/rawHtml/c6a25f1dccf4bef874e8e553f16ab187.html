KIRKWOOD\xc2\xa0\xe2\x80\xa2 Vianney football coach Paul Day experienced a first during the Golden Griffins\xe2\x80\x99 signing day ceremony on Wednesday morning.

Not only did nine Vianney football players sign national letter of intents to play for their respective universities, but three are headed to the same school \xe2\x80\x94 Truman State.

\xe2\x80\x9cI\xe2\x80\x99ve never had that,\xe2\x80\x9d Day said.

\n

Zach Zerwig, Devin Devres and Jack Borisenko are all headed to Truman.

Zerwig, a wide receiver, had a team-high 52 receptions for 672 yards and five touchdowns as a senior. He recorded 51 catches for 677 yards and three scores as a junior.

Zerwig\xe2\x80\x99s dad was a defensive lineman at Truman.

\xe2\x80\x9cJust the whole academics and football together and the family aspect of the football program felt like home to me,\xe2\x80\x9d Zerwig said.

Borisenko, a 6-foot-2, 305-pound offensive lineman, also has a family connection to Truman. He will be reunited with his brother, Connor, who also played for Vianney.

\xe2\x80\x9cHe was my host when I went out there,\xe2\x80\x9d Borisenko said. \xe2\x80\x9cHe showed me around and got me comfortable.\xe2\x80\x9d

Devres, a safety, is looking forward to the next level.

\xe2\x80\x9cIt\xe2\x80\x99s pretty cool because we already have good relationships and we\xe2\x80\x99re looking to form better relationships with everybody on the team,\xe2\x80\x9d Devres said. \xe2\x80\x9cI think we can bring a family atmosphere to the team.\xe2\x80\x9d

\n

Quarterback Tionne Harris signed with Quincy University.

Harris, a four-year starter, passed for 2,283 yards and 27 touchdowns as a senior and rushed for 1,129 yards and six touchdowns. He finished his four-year career with 8,175 passing yards with 78 touchdowns and 4,719 rushing yards with 38 scores.

\xe2\x80\x9cThere\xe2\x80\x99s no days off really,\xe2\x80\x9d Harris said. \xe2\x80\x9cI have to keep working hard to get better. There are other people already up there working hard right now, so I have to keep working to make sure I am ready for when I get up there.\xe2\x80\x9d

\n

Linebacker Jordan Guilfoy signed with Missouri State; defensive tackle Qadry Taalib signed with Robert Morris in Chicago; kicker Jimmy Lahrman signed with Missouri Valley College where he will also play baseball; offensive lineman Charles Springer signed with Benedictine University; and defensive tackle Tayvian Weary signed with Lindenwood University.

Guilfoy had 76 tackles and six sacks as a senior and 86 tackles and six sacks as a junior. Weary had 10 sacks as a senior and seven as a junior. Taalib had 46 tackles and 10 sacks as a senior and nine sacks as a junior.

Vianney football went 10-2 this season and 19-7 over the past two years, including winning the school\xe2\x80\x99s first state championship in 2016. This group of seniors won 15 of their final 17 games.

\xe2\x80\x9cI think the best thing that they\xe2\x80\x99ve been able to do is to change the culture and what the expectations are here,\xe2\x80\x9d Day said. \xe2\x80\x9cThey\xe2\x80\x99ve worked hard and they\xe2\x80\x99re a bunch of great kids that\xe2\x80\x99s for sure.\xe2\x80\x9d

Two Vianney soccer players also signed: forward Steven Bibas with SIU Edwardsville and goalkeeper TJ Reigner with Quincy.

Bibas had 13 goals, including 6 game-winners, in leading Vianney to a second place finish in the Class 4 state tournament last fall. He was also named the first All-American from Vianney in 25 years.

\xe2\x80\x9cIt\xe2\x80\x99s a blessing to receive this award, but I couldn\xe2\x80\x99t do it without my teammates, my coaches, my family and God,\xe2\x80\x9d Bibas said. \xe2\x80\x9cObviously this award means a lot to me, but the season we had means more to me.\xe2\x80\x9d
