Tributes have been paid to long-standing Rossington Main president Gerald Murden who died at the weekend aged 86.

\n\n\t\t\t\t

Main chairman Carl Stokes described \xe2\x80\x98Gerry\xe2\x80\x99 as \xe2\x80\x98one of non-league football\xe2\x80\x99s great characters\xe2\x80\x99.

\n
\n
\n

\xe2\x80\x9cGerald was fondly called Gerry by all who knew him,\xe2\x80\x9d said Carl. \xe2\x80\x9cHe would be seen down at our Oxford Street ground, not missing any of the matches until recent ill health restricted his attendance.

\n
\n
\n

\xe2\x80\x9cGerry would also be a regular down at the Rossington Miners Welfare club on a Thursday night for the weekly letter draw.

\n
\n
\n

\xe2\x80\x9cWhat will live in the memories of most would be Gerry running and serving behind the Tea cabin down at Main during each first and second team game.

\xe2\x80\x9cHe will be remembered as a Rossington Main legend and one of non-league football\xe2\x80\x99s great characters.

\xe2\x80\x9cOur thoughts go out to all his friends and family. He will be deeply missed. RIP Gerry.\xe2\x80\x9d

Assistant boss Lee Holmes tweeted: \xe2\x80\x9cOne of our own and one of the best thanks for everything RIP Gerry\xe2\x80\x9d

\n
\n
\n
\n
\n
\n

Coach Andy Cowe tweeted: \xe2\x80\x9cSmashing bloke who will be sorely missed. RIP, legend.\xe2\x80\x9d
